# Risk Management & Position Sizing

Most beginners obsess over *entries*. Professionals obsess over **risk**. You cannot control whether a trade wins; you can always control how much you lose if it doesn't.

**The core idea — risk a fixed small % per trade.** A common rule: risk **1%** (or less) of your account on any single trade.
- Account $100,000 → risk $1,000 per trade.
- If your stop is $5 below your $100 entry (you'll exit at $95), you're risking $5/share → buy **200 shares** ($1,000 ÷ $5). The position is $20,000, but your *risk* is $1,000.

**Stop-loss:** decide *before* you enter where you'd admit you're wrong, and exit there — mechanically. A stop turns an unlimited problem into a known, budgeted one.

**Think in R.** "R" = the amount you risked. A trade that makes 3× your risk is "+3R"; a loss is "−1R". Aim for setups where the potential reward is at least 2R for every 1R risked. Then you can be right less than half the time and still profit.

**Why position sizing beats everything:** a string of losses is inevitable. Risking 1% means 10 losses in a row is −10%; risking 20% means 5 losses nearly wipes you out. **Drawdown** (your worst peak-to-trough fall) is what ends accounts — protect against it.

> The app enforces a per-trade budget (`max_position_usd`) and a daily trade cap, and every backtest reports max drawdown — because surviving is the whole game.
