# Reading Candlesticks

Each candle summarizes one period (a day, an hour). It has four prices: **open, high, low, close**.

- The **body** runs from open to close. Green/hollow = close above open (buyers won). Red/filled = close below open (sellers won).
- The **wicks** (shadows) are the high and low — how far price stretched before snapping back.

**What the shape tells you:**
- **Long body** = strong conviction in that direction.
- **Long lower wick** (a *hammer*) = sellers pushed price down but buyers slammed it back up — a possible bottom.
- **Long upper wick** (a *shooting star*) = buyers ran out of steam — a possible top.
- **Tiny body / cross** (a *doji*) = open ≈ close — indecision, often near turning points.

**Two-candle patterns:**
- **Bullish engulfing**: a big green candle fully swallows the prior red one — momentum flipping up.
- **Bearish engulfing**: the opposite — momentum flipping down.

**The honest caveat:** patterns are *hints*, not guarantees. They work better at meaningful levels (support/resistance) and with volume confirmation. On their own they're closer to coin flips. Use them to ask "who's in control here?" — not as buy/sell buttons.
