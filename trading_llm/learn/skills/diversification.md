# Diversification & Correlation

Diversification is often called the only "free lunch" in investing: spreading money across things that *don't move together* reduces your risk more than it reduces your return.

**The key is correlation, not just count.**
- **Correlation** ranges from +1 (move identically) to −1 (move oppositely), with 0 meaning unrelated.
- Owning 10 tech stocks isn't diversified — they're highly correlated, so they crash together. Owning stocks + bonds + gold + crypto mixes lower-correlation assets, so a bad day for one is often cushioned by another.
- When a single position can sink your account, you're **concentrated**, not invested.

**Why it works:** if two assets each swing ±20% but don't move in sync, a 50/50 blend swings *less* than 20% — the bumps partly cancel. You keep most of the average return with less of the gut-wrenching volatility.

**The limits:**
- In a real panic, correlations spike toward +1 — "everything falls together" — so diversification helps least exactly when you want it most. That's why you still need position sizing and cash.
- Over-diversifying (owning hundreds of things) just buys you the index with extra effort. A handful of genuinely different bets is plenty.
