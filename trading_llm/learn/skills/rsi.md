# RSI — Relative Strength Index

RSI is a momentum **oscillator** that runs from 0 to 100. It compares the size of recent up-moves to recent down-moves over a window (usually 14 periods).

- **Above 70** = "overbought" — price has risen fast; a pause or pullback is more likely.
- **Below 30** = "oversold" — price has fallen fast; a bounce is more likely.
- **Around 50** = balanced.

**The beginner trap:** "overbought" does **not** mean "sell now." In a strong uptrend, RSI can sit above 70 for weeks while price keeps climbing. Shorting an overbought market in a strong trend is a classic way to lose money. RSI extremes are most useful in *range-bound* markets, not powerful trends.

**The sharper signal — divergence:**
- **Bullish divergence**: price makes a *lower* low but RSI makes a *higher* low → selling is losing steam.
- **Bearish divergence**: price makes a *higher* high but RSI makes a *lower* high → buying is losing steam.

Divergence is a heads-up that momentum and price disagree — often an early warning of a turn, but confirm it with price action before acting.
