# Crypto vs Stocks

Cryptocurrencies trade like assets but behave differently from stocks. The mechanics you've learned (bid/ask, support/resistance, RSI) still apply — but the context is wilder.

**Key differences:**
- **24/7 markets**: no opening bell, no close, no circuit breakers. Big moves happen at 3am on a weekend. You can't "wait for the market to open" to react.
- **Extreme volatility**: 5–10% daily swings are normal; −50% drawdowns happen. Position sizing matters *even more* here.
- **No earnings, no cash flow** (for most): a stock represents a slice of a profit-making business. A coin's value comes from supply/demand, network usage, and narrative — there's no P/E to anchor to.
- **You can self-custody**: holding your own keys means no broker can freeze you — but lose the keys and the money is gone forever. "Not your keys, not your coins."

**Crypto-native concepts you'll hear:**
- **On-chain data**: because the blockchain is public, you can see real flows (wallets, exchange in/outflows) — a transparency stocks don't offer.
- **Funding rate**: in perpetual futures, a periodic payment between longs and shorts; extreme funding signals crowded positioning (and likely a squeeze).
- **Stablecoins**: tokens pegged to $1, used as the "cash" of crypto.
