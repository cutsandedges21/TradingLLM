# Support, Resistance & Breakouts

Markets have memory. Prices where buyers repeatedly showed up form **support** (a floor); prices where sellers repeatedly showed up form **resistance** (a ceiling).

- **Support** = a level where falling prices have bounced before. Buyers see "cheap."
- **Resistance** = a level where rising prices have stalled before. Sellers see "expensive" or break-even.
- The **more times** a level is tested, the more traders watch it — which makes it more meaningful (until it breaks).

**Breakouts:** when price pushes decisively *through* resistance (or support) on strong volume, it often keeps going — old resistance frequently becomes new support (a "role reversal"). That's the logic behind momentum and "near-the-high" strategies.

**Fakeouts:** price pokes through a level, sucks in breakout traders, then snaps back. Painful and common. Volume helps separate the real thing (heavy participation) from the trap (thin, hesitant).

**How traders use it:**
- Buy near support / sell near resistance (range trading), **or** buy the breakout (momentum) — opposite styles, both valid in different markets.
- Place a **stop-loss** just beyond the level: if support breaks, your thesis is wrong — exit.

> Levels are zones, not exact prices. Don't treat a round number as a laser line; give it some breathing room.
